DID YOU KNOW???

January was the last month to have been added to the calendar, which originally had ten months and began in March. During his reign (c. 715-673), Numa revised the Roman calendar so that January replaced March as the first month of the year. It was deemed a fitting choice, since January was named after Janus - the Roman god of all beginnings and endings, who presided over doors and gates.

Janus is usually depicted with two faces - one looking forward and the other looking backward.
